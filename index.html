Kabocha Soup by Setsuko Yoshizuka

Ingredients

  • 1 pound kabocha pumpkin
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 2 teaspoons chicken bouillon powder, or 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups water, if using chicken bouillon
  • 1 2/3 cups milk
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Preparation

  • 1. Wash the exterior of the kabocha squash.
  • 2. Cut in half and remove seeds and inner strands of the squash.
  • 3. Place kabocha on a microwave-safe plate and heat in microwave for 1 to 2 minutes, depending on the power of your microwave. This helps to soften the kabocha just a bit.
  • 3. Cut the kabocha into thin slices and remove exterior skin. Set slices aside.
  • 4. In a medium pot, melt butter and sauté onion slices until translucent and softened.
  • 5. Add kabocha and sauté together with onions until the kabocha is cooked.
  • 6. Pour water and add chicken bouillon powder in the pot. (Note: Chicken broth may be Fresh chopped parsley, for garnish substituted for the bouillon and water.)
  • 7. Simmer on low heat for about 10 to 15 minutes, or until kabocha is softened.
  • 8. Either use an immersion hand blender to blend the vegetables with the broth, or allow the mixture to cool slightly and then pour it into a traditional blender to puree, then add it back to the pot.
  • 9. On medium-high heat, add milk to the kabocha mixture and bring to a boil, constantly stirring the soup. Turn off the heat, and season with salt and pepper. Mix thoroughly and serve immediately. Garnish with additional freshly ground black pepper and chopped parsley.
